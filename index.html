Summer Court

Culture: Arguably very rich in culture, even small villages are as decorated and aesthetically pleasing as Carnuri, Gorias' wealthy capital city. Buildings are most commonly grown straight out of the ground like massive trees, and would blend well with their forest surroundings if not for the high walls keeping residents in and unwelcome monsters of the Nightmare Court out. As wonderful as such a gorgeous place sounds it is very important to keep in mind that their entire culture is centered around the prejudice of external beauty. Appearance is everything, especially to the residents, who feel the need to compete with one another constantly. They make a game of who can be more decorated, especially in society.

History & Lore

Dubbed the most powerful of all the Firstborn Sidhes in Gorias, their stigma of "godhood" among the other Superior Races made it very easy for the smooth talking manipulative Oberon to convince his people to crown him as High King. Some may argue he had no right, but the Dream did still depict him as a leader, and when The Path was born many feared defying it. When the aspects of "good" and "evil" invaded, fearing for his own life the Monarch of Gorias fled the Faerie Realms, only returning when his spies told him that his own kind began their revolt. Feigning bravery he returned, using his magic to take back his home and clear out a safe space for Easterners once more.

Oberon's family is almost comically large, mostly due to his own greed and near limitless lust. The man has tons of half human offspring thanks to his wanderings when fleeing Xink influence in his home world, though none are considered legitimate or true heirs. Prior to the great wars and horrid invasions Oberon had a Firstborn Sidhe sister named Morgan, though she was personally targeted by enemies of Fae-kind and killed. Now he keeps a steady harem of ten wives, nameless Sidhe women in fancy garb and chains treated more like property than living beings. They continue to incubate and birth his children as the years pass by, ever increasing his number of Princes and Princesses, most of which are just as despicable in nature as their father.

Immortality
The First Generation Faeries were "born" into one single physical age and remain that age eternally. They can die from anything except natural causes.

Mana Sensitivity
The Fae-kind are very sensitive to auras and mana, allowing them to feel or see when another being is drawing from their mana pool or activating their mana, even if something is blocking their vision, a faint outline of an aura can be seen--or sensed--so long as the fellow magic user is within a twenty foot radius of the Fae. She cannot tell how much mana is being used or the user has left over, she is merely sensitive to when it is currently in use within the radius.

Enhanced Speed
The body of the Fae-kind is light, petite, and athletic. They can move double the speed of the average human and the strongest Fae's speeds can rival that of a hummingbird's.

Enhanced Flexibility
Their bodies are as flexible as they are light, allowing them to contort their shapes into absurd formations. Something someone would expect to see in a circus act.

Burning Calories
The Fae-kind eat thousands upon thousands of calories on purpose and burn them off very quickly. Physical sugary foods is said to help their mental and magic stability, often allowing them slightly faster mana regeneration than what is average if consumed when their mana-pool is either completely drained or close to it.

Hidden Wings
Tribal-like wing markings cover the backs of the Fae-kind. These tribal markings cause various different kinds of winged silhouettes to form as an aura behind each individual Faerie when they dip into their mana pool.

Advanced Perception
Rather than rise in the ranks of typical physical speed, her mind processes actions and comes up with a reaction twice as fast as the average human's.

Enhanced Strength
Years of physical training have paid off. Faeries always had the capacity to be physically strong despite their average dainty figures. This Faerie is now twice as strong as the average human, thanks to the years of hard work and the significant rise in power.
